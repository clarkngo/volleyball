## 1. Middle “2” (quick): same set tempo, different outcomes

**Concept:** One middle quick / “2-ball”–style situation, then branch or compare **different attack methods**, **approaches**, **blocker counts** (solo / double / triple), **weak perimeter vs solid defense**, **libero in vs out**, **setter constraints** — with **why** you’d choose each attack (tool, wipe, high hands, tip, etc.).

**Implemented as its own prototype?** No.

*Related shipped work (not a substitute):* [`prototypes/06-attack-sets.html`](../prototypes/06-attack-sets.html) — net zones 1–9, call-sheet sets, some middle labels; **no** branching narrative, block counts, or “why this attack.”

---

## 2. Serve receive: three back-row starters vs server profile

**Concept:** How to **ready** the **three back-row** passers given **where the server stands** (left vs right zone), **lefty vs righty** server, and serve type: **topspin standing**, **jump topspin**, **standing float**, **jump float**, **hybrid** — with recommended starting positions / weight distribution / lane priorities.

**Implemented as its own prototype?** No.

*Related shipped work (not a substitute):* [`prototypes/02-ball-arc.html`](../prototypes/02-ball-arc.html) — stylized **ball path** only; **no** reception formation or server handedness.

---

## 3. Left back defense: mental model over time

**Concept:** Playing **left back** defense — show a **thought process** (thinking bubbles or captions) with **nuances** (read block, seam, touch vs no-touch, high line, short roll, setter dump, etc.), advancing **per second or per sequence** so it feels like live scanning.

**Implemented as its own prototype?** No.

*Related shipped work (not a substitute):* [`prototypes/05-outside-hitter-defense.html`](../prototypes/05-outside-hitter-defense.html) — **perimeter vs rotational** D vs **outside hitter**, cones and rings; **not** LB-specific, **not** time-stepped internal monologue.

## 5. Setter footwork: out-of-system serve receive & first ball

**Concept:** **Footwork and step count** the setter needs for many **out-of-system** serve-receive and **first-ball** situations (platform inside, tight, off-net, chase, etc.) — patterns, not one perfect pass.

**Implemented as its own prototype?** No.

*Related shipped work (not a substitute):* [`prototypes/07-outside-hitter-approach.html`](../prototypes/07-outside-hitter-approach.html) — **hitter** approach vs **5 / 4 / Shoot**; **no** setter OOS footwork catalog.
