The Pass (or Bump)

What it is:

A pass, or "bump," is used to receive the opponent's serve or attack. The player joins their arms and uses their forearms to direct the ball to the setter.

Analogy for Beginners:

Think of passing as being a human "trampoline." Your arms create a flat, firm surface to bounce the ball high and accurately to your target, just like a trampoline launches a person into the air.
